What it does

Built around
real coaching

Every feature in KINETIC exists to make your training more accurate and more effective — not to look impressive in a demo.

Real-Time Pose Tracking
MediaPipe Pose Landmarker runs on every frame. 33 body landmarks tracked with visibility scoring and EMA angle smoothing to eliminate jitter.
Automatic Rep Counting
Joint angle thresholds detect up and down phases precisely. No button pressing — just perform the movement and KINETIC counts.
AI Form Analysis
Raw pose metrics are sent to an LLM which infers form issues from joint angles and flags — no hardcoded rules. The coaching adapts to what it actually sees.
Voice Coaching
Text-to-speech feedback delivered at the right moment — on set completion, when form breaks down, or when no pose is detected. Hands-free, eyes on the workout.
Progress Analytics
Daily workout history stored in SQLite. Reps, sets, and duration tracked per exercise per session. Bar charts show progress over time across exercises.
Browser-Native
No app install. No wearable. Just a webcam and a browser. Camera frames are processed server-side via WebSocket — session state persists through brief disconnects.
Process

From camera to
coaching in milliseconds

01
Set your workout
Choose an exercise, set your target sets and reps. No account needed — just enter a username and start.
02
Camera streams frames
Your webcam sends JPEG frames over a WebSocket to the server. Ack-based flow control ensures no lag buildup.
03
AI analyses pose
MediaPipe detects 33 landmarks per frame. EMA smoothing cleans the signal. Exercise-specific detectors count reps and flag form issues.
04
Coaching delivered
Raw metrics go to an LLM which decides what to say. Text-to-speech plays the feedback aloud while the annotated frame renders in your browser.

FAQ

Common
questions

No installation, no sign-up. KINETIC runs entirely in your browser. You enter a username (which identifies your workout history locally) and start training immediately. The only requirement is camera access permission.
Yes — frames are sent over a secure WebSocket to the backend where MediaPipe processes them server-side. Frames are not stored. Only the resulting rep count, set progress, and form metrics are returned to your browser. No video is saved at any point.
Raw joint angles and pose status flags (depth, alignment, swing, etc.) are extracted from MediaPipe and sent directly to a large language model (LLaMA-3.3-70B via Groq). The LLM analyses these numbers itself and decides what coaching cue to give — there are no hardcoded if/else rules for form detection.
Five exercises are currently supported with full form analysis: Squats, Push-ups, Biceps Curls (Dumbbell), Shoulder Press, and Lunges. Each has a dedicated detector with exercise-specific angle thresholds and status flags.
The app works fully without a Groq API key — you will have real-time pose tracking, rep counting, form metrics, and progress history. The voice coaching feature requires setting the GROQ_API_KEY environment variable before starting the server.
Completed sets are written to a local SQLite database (data.db) on the server, keyed by your username. The analytics endpoint groups these by exercise and date for the progress chart. Clearing the database resets all history.
